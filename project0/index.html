Artisan Bread

This recipe is a great recipe for those who've never made homemade bread before. Requiring only 4 ingredients, this recipe is perfect for those looking to learn more about bread making and yeast! While you could follow the no-knead method, learning and becoming comfortable with kneading and shaping dough will allow you to bake some of the best loaves you've ever tasted, and it will all be done by your own two hands.

Equipment

  • Banneton
  • Dutch Oven, Pizza stone, or oven-safe pot
  • Large/Deep mixing bowl
  • Tea towel/Proofing liner
  • Measuring cups and spoon (or scale)
  • Lame (or Razor for scoring)

Ingredients

  • 420g bread flour (3 1/4 cups)
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 2 teaspoons course salt
  • 1 1/2 cups (360ml)luke warm water

Instructions

  1. In your large/deep mixing bowl, mix together the flour, yeast, salt, and water. Feel free to use a wooden spoon or even better, your hands! Don't worry if the dough is sticky, this is totally normal. Once all the ingredients all well combined and incorporated, shape into a ball as best as you can.
  2. Cover well with a tea towel, apron, etc., for 2-3 hours at room temperature. The dough should almost double in size.
  3. Now, after this step, you could skip right to the baking part, no-knead required! However, for absolute best flavor, I highly suggest kneading your bread dough periodically (once or twice every hour until bake time should suffice). For an easy tutorial on kneading, Click here
  4. Roughly 1 whole hour before baking your bread, pre-heat your oven to 475 degrees F. Whichever tool you choose to bake your bead (I highly suggest a dutch-oven), don't forget to put that in the oven so it can heat up as well. During this time, shape your dough once more, and place it into your lightly-floured wooden banneton. Loosely cover until the 1 hour has passed.
  5. When this hour has passed, gently turn your banneton over onto a lightly-floured piece of parchment paper. Using your bread lame, razor, or scissors, score your dough with 3-4 slashes across the top, about 1/2 in deep. Once you become more confident, you can find more intricate scoring patterns online!
  6. Now, carefully remove your dutch-oven or oven-safe pot with cover, and gently place your dough (on the parchment) into the pot, and cover. Bake for 25 minutes at 475 degrees F, and then remove the lid and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Remove the bread from the oven and tap the bottom of the loaf carefully, if it sounds hollow, then the bread is done! If not, put it back in the oven, directly on the rack, for an additional few minutes.
  8. Let cool for at least 30-40 minutes before slicing and enjoying!

Tips and Notes

  • Flour: I personally recommend using a combination of white (70%), wheat (20%), and rye (10%) flour. The bread will be slightly more dense and packing with more flavor!
  • Yeast: If using instant yeast, you can mix the ingredients together, no problem! If using active dry yeast, you'll need to combine the water and yeast beforehand for a couple of minutes before adding other ingredients.
  • Dutch oven: I highly suggest using a dutch oven, as they can not only withstand warmer oven temperatures, but the lid will allow for a nice crust to form.
  • Water: It's better to use water that isn't too cold or too hot. If it's too cold, it will slow down the yeast development. Too hot and you could damage or kill your yeast.
